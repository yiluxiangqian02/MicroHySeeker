Normal Pulse Voltammetry Parameters dialog box

 

In Normal Pulse Voltammetry (NPV), the base potential is held at Init E, and a sequence of potential pulses with increasing amplitude is applied. The current at the end of each potential pulse is sampled and recorded as a function of the pulse potential. The following diagram shows the potential waveform applied as a function of time and the current sampling scheme.

Notes

  Init E and Final E should be at least 0.01 V apart.

  Pulse Width should be no more than half of Pulse Period; otherwise the system will automatically readjust Pulse Width.

  Sample Width should be no more than half of Pulse Width; otherwise the system will automatically readjust Sample Width.

  When the second electrode is set to Scan, its potential will be the same as the primary electrode; it is not independent.
