Differential Pulse Voltammetry Parameters dialog box

 

In Differential Pulse Voltammetry (DPV), the base potential is incremented from Init E toward Final E. A potential pulse is applied. Current is sampled before the potential pulse and at the end of the pulse. The difference between these two current samples is recorded as a function of potential. The following diagram shows the potential waveform applied as a function of time and the current sampling scheme.

Notes

  Init E and Final E should be at least 0.01 V apart.

  Pulse Width should be no more than half of Pulse Period; otherwise the system will automatically readjust Pulse Width.

  Sample Width should be no more than half of Pulse Width; otherwise the system will automatically readjust Sample Width.

  When Amplitude is negative, the pulse direction will in the opposite direction of the potential scan.

  When the second electrode is set to Scan, its potential will be the same as the primary electrode; it is not independent.
